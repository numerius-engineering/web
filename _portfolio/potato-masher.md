    <h2>Project focus</h2>
    <p>The build began as a practical repair problem: replacing a failed wooden handle with a fabricated alternative that could be assembled around the original masher shank. The design used a 3D-printed clamshell approach and plastic welding to create a custom-fit replacement with minimal turnaround time.</p>
    <h2>What it demonstrates</h2>
    <p>This project highlights the value of rapid fabrication for household tools and one-off functional parts. It also demonstrates an important engineering lesson: real-world use conditions matter. While the concept worked in service for a period of time, exposure to dishwasher heat ultimately revealed the thermal limits of the chosen material and informed what a stronger next iteration would require.</p>
